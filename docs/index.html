my-AI-scene

A local-first, verification-driven video production engine. A human authors a ProductionSpec; local HuggingFace models synthesize the narration, footage, and music inside it; pure-Python verifiers decide what survives to the final cut.

Same thesis, third medium.

What it does

A production guide becomes a graded, assembled MP4.

Where my-AI-story turns a series bible into a multi-voice audio episode, my-AI-scene turns a production guide into a scored, color-graded, assembled video — under the same Deterministic Scaffold thesis: human-owned constraints → AI synthesis → deterministic verification at every boundary. It's the engine behind The Journal of Informal Human Protocols, the MoreSalamander mockumentary series.

ProductionSpec  →  narration · footage · music  →  verify each boundary  →  grade + assemble  →  episode.mp4
The naming trio. my-AI-stro (the conductor) · my-AI-story (the written episode) · my-AI-scene (the visual cut). And my-AI-script writes the ProductionSpec that feeds this engine.

The boundaries it verifies

A model proposes each layer; a gate scores it.

Every stage has a deterministic check — the gate is never an LLM. Narration is transcribed and matched against the script; visuals are scored for relevance; music is a non-blocking bed; the final cut is assembled only from what passed. The model fills the frame; Python decides whether the frame survives.
